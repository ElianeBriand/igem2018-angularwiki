Thanks to medicinal advances, each year drug consumption increases to meet the needs of the population.

However drugs are not totally metabolized and by-products accumulate in wastewater.

A majority of studies on the subject are done in countries that already have a wastewater treatment program.
Pharmaceuticals pollution is underated.
If no mitigating action is taken, the environmental threat could increase by up to 65% in 2050 for some drugs.
OUR SOLUTION: METHOTREXIT
o
We designed a unique biotransformation pathway using a heterologously expressed carboxypeptidase coupled with FolC in Escherichia coli. MethotrExit drastically and quickly removes methotrexate, a broadly used anticancer drug, from the media.
We found degrading drugs can be toxic to the bacteria itself and we wanted a project that can be adaptable to any drug degradation in wastewater
We bring heterogeneity in the form of varying pathway expression, as that ensures the survival of a self-renewable reserve subpopulation :
A division of labor between ‘cleaning’ and ‘stem’ bacteria
