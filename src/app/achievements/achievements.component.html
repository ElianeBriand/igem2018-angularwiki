Integrated Human Practices

At the beginning of our project, two members of our team wanted to work on endocrine disruptor in wastewater treatment. However, several survey and research made on the subject indicated us that there were already plenty of solution for this kind of chemical. At that time, another idea popped up in our head.

What about anticancer drugs?

Indeed, cancer is one of the biggest diseases of our century, lots of people are affected by it, hence, lots of prescriptions, drugs are taken in other to fight this disease. Among does drugs, some really powerful one, which can cause dramatic effect on the environment. With that in mind, we decided to send surveys to the two biggest cancer research facilities in France and Europe. The answer was glaring. There was still plenty of job to do in this field.

In order to be sure to not waste time, and target the right drug to degrade, we also asked, on the “type” of drugs, researcher were working on the most. Finally, the methotrexate (MTX) appeared.

This is how our project was born.

To be sure, that our project was really integrated to the society we live in, we also asked to Arvia, a huge waste water treatment company, geolocalized in Britany to answer couple of our question about waste water drug treatment. Finally, by analyzing the answers to our surveys we propose to extend our project in at least three directions: i) the design of a bacteria confinement that could allow the use of the ‘cleaning factory’ at home for people on outpatient treatment, ii) the use of our system as a probiotic in the case of MTX acute toxicity, and iii) the use of the ‘heterogenious’ expression system we develop for this project in other strains use in biotechnologies and in which the synthetic pathways of interest behave as parasitic systems and are associated to fitness-cost.

Model

Advanced models were created and used to get insight into the core of the project, and the degradation of the MTX. More precisely, we modelize the case in which the degradation of anticancer drugs and the biotransformation pathways themselves are toxic. Modeling an heterogeneous expression of the synthetic pathway within the ‘cleaning factory’ population highlights the interest of a division of labor between “cleaning” and “stem” bacterial cells.

Demonstrate our project work

We designed synthetic cassettes encoding a new biotransformation pathway using an heterologous carboxypeptidase in Escherichia coli. In only 5 h, our ‘cleaning factory’ drastically removes MTX from the media. During our project we demonstrated that the level of MTX after getting through our E. coli population, dropped from 512 µM to close to 0, proving that our degrading system is efficient.

Improvement of an existing biobrick

During the part of our part concerning the ‘heterogenious’ expression system, we initially wanted to express ler genes at different levels. Therefore we had interest in the large and very well characterized Anderson promoter collection. Four promoters from this collection were available in pSB1C3 backbone vector in the Distribution Kit 2018 (BBa_J23119 ; BBa_J23108 ; BBa_J23109 ; BBa_J23111). By sequencing the pSB1C3 plasmids carrying these promoters we identified in three of them extra-sequences (notable SpeI extra-sites) that were detrimental for further Biobrick assembly strategy. We corrected one of them by directed mutagenesis and sent the corrected pSB1C3-BBa_J23108_corrected plasmid ( BBa_K2688025) to iGEM.
