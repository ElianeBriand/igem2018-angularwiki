HeteroGenious

Context

Non-genetic phenotypic heterogeneity is present in large-scope in bacterial population. In enteropathogenic Escherichia coli (EPEC), the expression of the locus of enterocyte effacement (LEE) pathogenicity island is controlled by the interplay of Ler (LEE encoded regulator) and H-NS (naturally expressed by all E. coli strains). This interplay results in the coexistence of nonvirulent and hypervirulent subpopulations.

Figure 1 : Interplay between Ler and H-NS for the modulation of LEE5 promoter activity
Ler is a nucleoid associated protein encoded by LEE (locus of enterocyte effacement) genomic island encoding a secretion system in enteropathogenic E. coli. For the project, we used ler and its target DNA, a small part of LEE island containing the promoter region of the 5th operon, named LEE5. This region has been shown to be recognized by two nucleoid-associated proteins, H-NS and Ler.  H-NS and Ler belong to the same family of protein of proteins qualified as ‘silencers’ of foreign DNA expression, the H-NS family. However, it has been reported that Ler can counteract the H-NS silencing on the LEE island.

We get inspiration from this natural system for creating our HeteroGenious device for the methotrexate (MTX) project. The idea was to propose a new genetic system, very simple (only two parts in E. coli) that can easily generate phenotypic heterogeneity within a clonal population with some bacteria that will significantly express the biotranformation pathway and other that will not and will ensure bacterial renewal within a bioreactor.

Toward a ‘HeteroGenious’ expression of BioBricks

While analysing the growth of our chassis strain (WT and ΔtolC strain) in absence or presence of MTX, we noticed that the strains harboring pSB1C3-folC-cpg2 present a growth delay/defect. More generally, BioBrick expression and/or drug degradation may be toxic for the chassis strains, and can be detrimental for the long term maintenance of a cleaning bioreactor ; anti-cancer drugs degradation pathways may be especially affected, given these drugs target very basic mecanism of life, likely to be conserved from bacteria to man.

Therefore we wanted our bioreactor to harbor a heterogeneous bacterial population so that all of them will not express the degradation pathway at the same time. This system is so called ‘HeteroGenious’ since it generates ‘ingenious’ heterogeneity within the ‘cleaning factory’ population.

We took advantage of a recently described regulation system that can generate bimodal expression patterns in E. coli . Only two components must be provided to implement this ‘heterogenious’ system in E. coli: LEE5 promoter region and the gene encoding Ler (‘LEE encoded regulator’). The E. coli endogeneous H-NS silencer also plays a part in the system : the competition between H-NS and Ler for LEE5 binding can generate some heterogeneity of transgene expression within E. coli populations (Figure 8).

Since H-NS is naturally present in E. coli, our synthetic system requires only the expression of Ler and a transgene under the control of LEE5. We analyzed the expression of gfp under the control of LEE5 promoter as proof of concept. The resulting population was heterogeneous regarding GFP expression since one sub-population expressed GFP and the other didn’t (Figure 9).

Depending on which protein (H-NS or LER) cooperatively binds to the LEE5 promoter, gfp expression is silenced or activated. Using this interesting property, several possibilities are opened for application to our project. Instead of gfp transgene, cpg2 and folC encoding genes could be clones in operon under the control of LEE5. This way, part of the population will not express the MTX-degradation pathway and therefosre will ensure cell renewal in the bioreactor. This illustrates the concept of ‘division of labor’ between ‘cleaning’ and ‘stem-like’ bacteria. All together, we can combine the degradation pathway with our heterogeneous expression device to achieve efficient degradation and long-term maintenance of the ‘Cleaning Factory’.
