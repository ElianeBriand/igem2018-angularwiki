HeteroGenious

Toward a ‘HeteroGenious’ expression of BioBricks

While analysing the growth of our chassis strain (WT and ΔtolC strain) in absence or presence of MTX, we noticied that the strains harboring pSB1C3-folC-cpg2 present a growth delay/defect. More generally, BioBrick expression and/or drug degradation may be toxic for the chassis strains, and can be detrimental for the long term maintenance of a cleaning bioreactor ; anti-cancer drugs degradation pathways may be especially affected, given these drugs target very basic mecanism of life, likely to be conserved from bacteria to man.

Therefore we wanted our bioreactor to harbor a heterogeneous bacterial population so that all of them will not express the degradation pathway at the same time. This system is so called ‘HeteroGenious’ since it generates ‘ingenious’ heterogeneity within the ‘cleaning factory’ population.

We took advantage of a recently described regulation system that can generate bimodal expression patterns in E. coli. Only two components are required to implement this ‘heterogenious’ system in E. coli: LEE5 promoter region and the gene encoding Ler (‘LEE encoded regulator’). Ler is a nucleoid associated protein encoded by LEE (locus of enterocyte effacement) genomic island encoding a secretion system in enteropathogenic E. coli. For the project, we used ler and its target DNA, a small part of LEE island containing the promoter region of the 5th operon, named LEE5. This region has been shown to be recognized by two nucleoid-associated proteins, H-NS and Ler.  H-NS and Ler belong to the same family of protein of proteins qualified as ‘silencers’ of foreign DNA expression, the H-NS family. However, it has been reported that Ler can counteract the H-NS silencing on the LEE island. Therefore, the competition between H-NS and Ler for LEE5 binding can generate some heterogeneity of transgene expression within E. coli populations (Figure 8).

Since H-NS is naturally present in E. coli, our synthetic system requires only the expression of Ler and a transgene under the control of LEE5. We analyzed the expression of gfp under the control of LEE5 promoter as proof of concept. The resulting population was heterogeneous regarding GFP expression since one sub-population expressed GFP and the other didn’t (Figure 9).
